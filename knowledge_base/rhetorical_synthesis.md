Creating a comprehensive study guide for Rhetorical Synthesis involves several key concepts, definitions, formulas, problem-solving approaches, common mistakes, and practice problems with solutions. Here’s a step-by-step approach to help you create an effective study guide:

### Key Concepts

1. **Rhetorical Analysis**: The process of analyzing the meaning or effect of language in a text.
2. **Rhetorical Devices**: Techniques used by writers to convey their message, such as allusion, metaphor, simile, and personification.
3. **Rhetorical Questions**: Questions that can be asked about the rhetorical devices used in a text.

### Important Formulas

1. **Rhetorical Analysis Formula**: The formula for analyzing the meaning of language is: "The meaning of a word or phrase is determined by its context."
2. **Rhetorical Devices Formula**: The formula for identifying rhetorical devices is: "A rhetorical device is an expression that serves to persuade, entertain, or comment on a topic."

### Common Mistakes

1. **Misinterpretation**: Recognize the difference between figurative and literal language.
2. **Overgeneralization**: Avoid making generalizations about all texts without evidence.
3. **Falsely identifying rhetorical devices**: Be careful not to confuse them with other literary devices.

### Practice Problem Types

- **Problem 1:** Identify the rhetorical device used in the following sentence: "The sun was shining brightly, and the birds were singing."
- **Problem 2:** Analyze the meaning of the following phrase: "He is a great leader."

### Practice Problems with Solutions

#### Problem 1
**Solution:**
The rhetorical device used here is allusion. The speaker uses the word "shining" to compare the sun's brightness to the birds' singing, which creates an image of the sun as shining brightly.

#### Problem 2
**Solution:**
The rhetorical device used in this sentence is personification. The speaker describes the birds as "singing," which is a personification because they are being described as having voices.

**Solution:**
What rhetorical device is being used here? The rhetorical question could be, "Why does the speaker say that he is a great leader?" This question asks for an explanation or justification for the speaker's statement.
