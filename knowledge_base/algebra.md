### Summary of the Knowledge Base Content:
1. **Historical Context & Evolution:** Traces the development of algebra from Rhetorical to Syncopated and Symbolic phases, detailing key historical contributions from Al-Khwarizmi, Diophantus, Omar Khayyam, Cardano, Viète, Descartes, and Galois.
2. **Foundational Terminology & Number Systems:** Clarifies definitions (constants, expressions, equations, etc.) and visualizes the complex and real number hierarchy using a **Mermaid** chart.
3. **The Axiomatic Framework (Field Axioms):** Outlines the commutative, associative, distributive, identity, and inverse rules that govern addition and multiplication.
4. **Essential Rules & Formulas Reference:** Contains ready-to-use LaTeX tables/lists for exponent rules, logarithm properties, and factoring identities (differences/sums of squares and cubes).
5. **Solving Equations & Inequalities:** Explains how to solve linear systems (with Cramer's rule/determinants), quadratic equations (using the discriminant chart), higher-degree polynomials (using the Fundamental Theorem of Algebra and factor/remainder theorems), and absolute value equations.
6. **Functions & Graphical Transformations:** Summarizes functional transformations (shifts, reflections, stretches/compressions) in a matrix.
7. **Advanced Algebraic Concepts:** Introduces abstract algebra structures (groups, rings, fields) with a hierarchy diagram, alongside sequences/series (arithmetic, geometric, binomial theorem) and linear algebra matrices (eigenvalues, determinants).
8. **Common Pitfalls & Diagnostics:** Explores the "Freshman's Dream" mistake, negative square roots, illegal cancellations, division-by-zero proofs, and inequality flips, finishing with a quick student checklist.
