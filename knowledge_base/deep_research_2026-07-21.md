### 2. **Reading Comprehension**
#### Key Concepts and Definitions
- **Main Idea**: The central theme or message of a passage.
- **Supporting Details**: Information that helps explain or support the main idea.
- **Inference**: Drawing conclusions based on the information provided.
- **Author's Purpose**: The reason the author wrote the passage.
- **Context Clues**: Words or phrases that help understand the meaning of unfamiliar words.

#### Important Formulas or Principles
- **None specific to Reading Comprehension**, but key strategies include:
  - **Skimming**: Quickly reading to get the gist of the passage.
  - **Close Reading**: Carefully reading and analyzing the text.
  - **Annotating**: Writing notes or questions while reading.

#### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Overlooking the main idea**.
- **Misinterpreting the author's purpose**.
- **Making assumptions not supported by the text**.
- **Failing to use context clues**.

#### Practice Problem Types
1. **Identifying the Main Idea**: What is the main idea of the passage?
2. **Finding Supporting Details**: Which sentence best supports the main idea?
3. **Making Inferences**: What can be inferred from the passage?
4. **Author's Purpose**: Why did the author write this passage?

### 3. **Writing and Language**
#### Key Concepts and Definitions
- **Sentence Structure**: The arrangement of words and phrases to create well-formed sentences.
- **Punctuation**: The use of marks to clarify meaning.
- **Grammar**: The rules governing the structure of sentences.
- **Style**: The author's choice of words and sentence structures.
- **Conventions**: Standard rules and practices in writing.

#### Important Formulas or Principles
- **Sentence Fragments**: A group of words that is not a complete sentence.
- **Run-on Sentences**: Two or more independent clauses joined without proper punctuation.
- **Commas**: Used to separate elements in a list, before conjunctions in compound sentences, and to set off non-essential information.
- **Pronoun-Antecedent Agreement**: Ensuring that pronouns agree with their antecedents in number and gender.

#### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Using fragments or run-on sentences**.
- **Incorrect comma usage**.
- **Misusing pronouns**.
- **Failing to maintain subject-verb agreement**.

#### Practice Problem Types
1. **Improving Sentence Structure**: Which sentence is correctly structured?
2. **Correcting Punctuation**: Which sentence uses commas correctly?
3. **Fixing Fragments and Run-ons**: Which sentence is a complete sentence?
4. **Maintaining Subject-Verb Agreement**: Which sentence has the correct subject-verb agreement?
