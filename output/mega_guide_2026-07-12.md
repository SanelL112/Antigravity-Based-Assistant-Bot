### 1.1 Heart of Algebra (Linear Equations, Systems, Inequalities) ⭐

#### Core Formulas & Theorems

**Linear Equations in Two Variables**
$$ax + by = c \quad \text{(Standard Form)}$$
$$y = mx + b \quad \text{(Slope-Intercept Form)}$$
$$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \quad \text{(Point-Slope Form)}$$

**Slope Formula**
$$m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}$$

**Parallel Lines**: $m_1 = m_2$  
**Perpendicular Lines**: $m_1 \cdot m_2 = -1$ (negative reciprocals)

**Systems of Linear Equations**
- **Substitution**: Solve one equation for a variable, substitute into the other
- **Elimination**: Add/subtract equations to eliminate a variable
- **Graphical**: Intersection point = solution

**Number of Solutions**:
| System Type | Lines | Solutions |
|-------------|-------|-----------|
| Consistent Independent | Intersecting | Exactly 1 |
| Consistent Dependent | Coincident (same line) | Infinitely many |
| Inconsistent | Parallel (distinct) | 0 |

**Linear Inequalities**
- Graph boundary line: **solid** for $\le, \ge$ | **dashed** for $<, >$
- Test point $(0,0)$ to determine shading side
- System of inequalities → **feasible region** (intersection of half-planes)

- **Day 1 HW**: $r = \frac{3}{5}f + 8$ (rabbits as function of foxes) → linear modeling
- **Day 1 Problem #7**: Line through origin, $(r,4)$, $(-1,s)$ → slope relationships
- **Day 24 Delta**: Systems of linear equations (substitution, elimination, graphing)
- **Problem-Solving 4/5/6/7**: Rates, Proportions, Ratios, Percent → all linear applications

#### Step-by-Step Strategy: SAT Linear Word Problems
1. **Identify variables**: What changes? What's constant?
2. **Write equations**: Translate sentences → algebra
3. **Choose method**: Substitution (one variable isolated) vs. Elimination (coefficients match)
4. **Solve & interpret**: Check units, answer the *specific question asked*
5. **Verify**: Plug back into context

#### Common Traps
- ❌ **Units mismatch**: mph vs. mpm, dollars vs. cents
- ❌ **Wrong intercept**: $b$ in $y=mx+b$ is $y$-intercept, not $x$-intercept
- ❌ **Inequality direction**: Flip sign when multiplying/dividing by negative
- ❌ **"No solution" vs "infinite solutions"**: Check if equations are multiples

#### Practice Problem 1 (from Day 1 context)
> The number of rabbits $r$ in a forest as a function of foxes $f$ is modeled by $r = \frac{3}{5}f + 8$. If a scientist counts 20 foxes and 18 rabbits in 2015, and 25 foxes and 30 rabbits in 2020, does the model perfectly fit the data? If not, what is the residual for 2020?

**Solution**:
- 2015: $r = \frac{3}{5}(20) + 8 = 12 + 8 = 20$ → observed 18, residual = $18 - 20 = -2$
- 2020: $r = \frac{3}{5}(25) + 8 = 15 + 8 = 23$ → observed 30, residual = $30 - 23 = +7$
- Model **does not fit perfectly**; residuals are non-zero. The relationship may not be perfectly linear or there are other factors.

### 1.2 Problem Solving & Data Analysis (PSDA) ⭐

#### Core Concepts & Formulas

**Ratios & Proportions**
$$\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d} \implies ad = bc \quad \text{(Cross-multiplication)}$$
**Unit Rate**: $\frac{\text{quantity}}{\text{1 unit}}$ (e.g., miles per hour, price per pound)

**Percentages**
$$\text{Part} = \text{Percent} \times \text{Whole} \quad (P = p \cdot W)$$
$$\text{Percent Change} = \frac{\text{New} - \text{Old}}{\text{Old}} \times 100\%$$
**Successive Percents**: Multiply factors (e.g., 20% increase → $\times 1.2$; then 10% decrease → $\times 0.9$; net = $1.2 \times 0.9 = 1.08 = 8\%$ increase)

**Rates & Work Problems**
$$\text{Rate} = \frac{\text{Work}}{\text{Time}} \quad \text{or} \quad \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}}$$
**Combined Rate**: $R_{\text{combined}} = R_1 + R_2$ (working together)
$$\frac{1}{T_{\text{combined}}} = \frac{1}{T_1} + \frac{1}{T_2}$$

**Statistics (from 8_Statistics.pdf & Problem-Solving 9)**
- **Mean**: $\bar{x} = \frac{\sum x_i}{n}$
- **Median**: Middle value (or average of two middle)
- **Mode**: Most frequent
- **Range**: $\text{Max} - \text{Min}$
- **Standard Deviation** (conceptual): Spread around mean; SAT tests *interpretation*, not calculation
- **Margin of Error**: $\text{Estimate} \pm \text{MoE}$ → confidence interval
- **Random Sampling vs. Random Assignment**: Sampling → generalizability; Assignment → causation
- **Correlation ≠ Causation**: SAT loves this distinction

**Probability**
$$P(A) = \frac{\text{favorable outcomes}}{\text{total outcomes}}$$
$$P(A \text{ and } B) = P(A) \cdot P(B|A) \quad \text{(dependent)}$$
$$P(A \text{ or } B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \text{ and } B)$$
**Independent**: $P(B|A) = P(B)$

**Scatterplots & Lines of Best Fit**
- **Slope** = rate of change (units: $y$-units per $x$-unit)
- **$y$-intercept** = predicted $y$ when $x=0$
- **$R^2$** (coefficient of determination): % variation in $y$ explained by $x$

#### Classroom Connection
- **Problem-Solving 4/5/6/7**: 4_Proportion.pdf, 5_Rates.pdf, 6_Ratios and Rates.pdf, 7_Percent.pdf + answer keys
- **Day 24 Delta**: Shipping rate tables, monthly fees (mean), wage increase (percent), geometric sequences
- **Day 28 Delta**: Cell growth $y = 16(2)^t$ (exponential), LCM, rectangular prism volume

#### Step-by-Step: Percent Change Problems
1. Identify **original (old)** and **new** values
2. Compute difference: $\text{New} - \text{Old}$
3. Divide by **original**: $\frac{\text{Diff}}{\text{Old}}$
4. Convert to percent: $\times 100\%$
5. **Watch for**: "percent less than" vs "percent of" — different denominators!

#### Common Traps
- ❌ **Base confusion**: "20% more than 50" = 60; "20% of 50" = 10
- ❌ **Successive percents**: Don't add/subtract percents directly; multiply factors
- ❌ **Sampling bias**: Convenience sample ≠ random sample
- ❌ **Extrapolation**: Predicting outside data range is unreliable

#### Practice Problem 2 (from Day 24 Delta)
> Jorge's hourly wage increases by 6%. If his original wage was $W$, which expression represents his new wage?
> A) $W + 0.06$  B) $W + 0.06W$  C) $1.06W$  D) Both B and C

**Solution**: **D**. 6% increase = original + 6% of original = $W + 0.06W = 1.06W$. Both B and C are equivalent.

### 1.3 Passport to Advanced Math (Quadratics, Polynomials, Exponentials) ⭐

#### Quadratic Functions (from 16_Quadratics.pdf, 11_Quadratics.pdf)

**Standard Form**: $f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c$
- $a > 0$ → opens up (minimum); $a < 0$ → opens down (maximum)
- $y$-intercept = $c$
- Axis of symmetry: $x = -\frac{b}{2a}$
- Vertex: $\left(-\frac{b}{2a}, f\left(-\frac{b}{2a}\right)\right)$

**Vertex Form**: $f(x) = a(x-h)^2 + k$ → vertex = $(h,k)$

**Factored Form**: $f(x) = a(x-r_1)(x-r_2)$ → roots/zeros = $r_1, r_2$

**Quadratic Formula**: $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$

**Discriminant**: $\Delta = b^2 - 4ac$
- $\Delta > 0$ → 2 distinct real roots
- $\Delta = 0$ → 1 real root (double root, vertex on x-axis)
- $\Delta < 0$ → 0 real roots (complex)

**Sum/Product of Roots** (Vieta's Formulas):
- Sum: $r_1 + r_2 = -\frac{b}{a}$
- Product: $r_1 r_2 = \frac{c}{a}$

**Vertex x-coordinate from sum of roots**: 
If sum of roots = 8, then $-\frac{b}{a} = 8 \implies \frac{-b}{2a} = 4$ → vertex at $x = 4$.
*(Matches Day 1 assignment: "If sum of roots is 8, x-coordinate of vertex is 4")*

#### Polynomials
- **Degree** = highest exponent
- **End behavior**: Leading term dominates ($ax^n$)
- **Remainder Theorem**: $f(c)$ = remainder when $f(x)$ divided by $(x-c)$
- **Factor Theorem**: $(x-c)$ is a factor $\iff f(c) = 0$

#### Exponential Functions
$$f(x) = a \cdot b^x \quad (a \neq 0, b > 0, b \neq 1)$$
- $b > 1$ → growth; $0 < b < 1$ → decay
- **Percent change**: $b = 1 + r$ (growth) or $b = 1 - r$ (decay)
- **Doubling time / Half-life**: Solve $b^t = 2$ or $b^t = 0.5$

#### Classroom Connection
- **Problem-Solving 5/6/7**: 16_Quadratics.pdf, 11_Quadratics.pdf + answer keys
- **Day 1 Assignment**: "If sum of roots of quadratic is 8, find x-coordinate of vertex" → **4**
- **Day 28 Delta**: Cell growth $y = 16(2)^t$ → exponential modeling ($t=5 \to y=16 \cdot 32 = 512$)

#### Step-by-Step: Quadratic Word Problems
1. **Identify model**: Area → $A = x( \text{length} )$; Projectile → $h(t) = -16t^2 + v_0t + h_0$
2. **Find vertex** for max/min (area, height, profit)
3. **Find roots** for when value = 0 (hits ground, breaks even)
4. **Use symmetry**: Vertex is midpoint of roots

#### Common Traps
- ❌ **Wrong form**: Don't use standard form when vertex form is obvious
- ❌ **Discriminant sign**: $b^2 - 4ac$ not $4ac - b^2$
- ❌ **Exponential vs. linear**: Constant *ratio* → exponential; constant *difference* → linear
- ❌ **Vertex x-coordinate**: $-\frac{b}{2a}$, not $\frac{b}{2a}$

#### Practice Problem 3
> A quadratic has roots summing to 8. What is the x-coordinate of its vertex?
> **Answer**: 4 (vertex x = midpoint of roots = sum/2 = 8/2 = 4)

> A ball is thrown with height $h(t) = -16t^2 + 64t + 5$. Find max height and when it hits ground.
> **Max height**: Vertex at $t = -\frac{64}{2(-16)} = 2$ sec; $h(2) = -16(4) + 128 + 5 = 69$ ft
> **Hits ground**: Solve $-16t^2 + 64t + 5 = 0$ → $t = \frac{-64 \pm \sqrt{4096 + 320}}{-32} = \frac{-64 \pm \sqrt{4416}}{-32} \approx 4.07$ sec

### 1.4 Additional Topics: Geometry, Trigonometry, Complex Numbers ⭐

#### Circles (from 19_Circles.pdf — Problem-Solving 9) 🔴 CURRENT FOCUS

**Equation of a Circle**
$$(x-h)^2 + (y-k)^2 = r^2 \quad \text{center } (h,k), \text{ radius } r$$
**General Form**: $x^2 + y^2 + Dx + Ey + F = 0$ → complete the square to find center/radius

**Arc Length & Sector Area** (radians!)
$$\text{Arc Length} = r\theta \quad (\theta \text{ in radians})$$
$$\text{Sector Area} = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$$
**Conversion**: $180^\circ = \pi \text{ rad}$ → $\theta_{\text{rad}} = \theta_{\text{deg}} \cdot \frac{\pi}{180}$

**Central Angle = Arc Measure** (in degrees or radians)
**Inscribed Angle** = $\frac{1}{2} \times \text{intercepted arc}$

**Chord Properties**
- Perpendicular from center bisects chord
- Equal chords are equidistant from center

**Tangent Properties**
- Tangent $\perp$ radius at point of tangency
- Two tangents from external point are equal in length

**Power of a Point**
- Secant-Secant: $PA \cdot PB = PC \cdot PD$
- Secant-Tangent: $PA \cdot PB = PT^2$

#### Triangles (Day 7 HW: 16_Lines and Angles.pdf, 17_Triangles.pdf)

**Angle Sum**: $180^\circ$
**Exterior Angle** = sum of two remote interior angles

**Triangle Inequality**: For sides $a,b,c$: $a + b > c$, $a + c > b$, $b + c > a$
→ Third side: $|a-b| < c < a+b$
*(Day 1: sides 9 and 6 → perimeter integer values: $3 < x < 15 \implies x \in \{4,5,...,14\} \implies P \in \{19,20,...,29\}$)*

**Special Right Triangles**
- **45-45-90**: $x, x, x\sqrt{2}$ (legs equal, hyp = leg$\sqrt{2}$)
- **30-60-90**: $x, x\sqrt{3}, 2x$ (short leg, long leg, hyp)

**Pythagorean Theorem**: $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$
**Pythagorean Triples**: 3-4-5, 5-12-13, 8-15-17, 7-24-25 (and multiples)

**Area**: $\frac{1}{2}bh$ or $\frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$ (SAS)

**Similar Triangles**: Corresponding angles equal → sides proportional
- **AA, SAS, SSS** similarity criteria
- Ratio of areas = (scale factor)$^2$

#### Trigonometry (SAT: Right Triangle Only; ACT: Unit Circle + Oblique)

**SOH-CAH-TOA** (Right Triangles)
$$\sin \theta = \frac{\text{opp}}{\text{hyp}}, \quad \cos \theta = \frac{\text{adj}}{\text{hyp}}, \quad \tan \theta = \frac{\text{opp}}{\text{adj}}$$

**Complementary Angles**: $\sin \theta = \cos(90^\circ - \theta)$

**Unit Circle** (ACT)
- $(\cos \theta, \sin \theta)$ on circle radius 1
- Key angles: $0, 30, 45, 60, 90^\circ$ ($\frac{\pi}{6}, \frac{\pi}{4}, \frac{\pi}{3}, \frac{\pi}{2}$ rad)

**Law of Sines** (ACT): $\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}$

**Law of Cosines** (ACT): $c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab\cos C$

#### Complex Numbers (SAT)
$$i = \sqrt{-1}, \quad i^2 = -1, \quad i^3 = -i, \quad i^4 = 1$$
**Standard Form**: $a + bi$
**Conjugate**: $a - bi$
**Multiplication**: $(a+bi)(c+di) = (ac-bd) + (ad+bc)i$
**Division**: Multiply numerator/denominator by conjugate of denominator
$$|a+bi| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2} \quad \text{(modulus)}$$

#### Coordinate Geometry
**Distance**: $d = \sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2 + (y_2-y_1)^2}$
**Midpoint**: $\left(\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1+y_2}{2}\right)$
**Slope**: $\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}$

### 2.3 Writing: Standard English Conventions (Grammar, Punctuation, Sentence Structure)

#### Grammar Rules (from 8_W, 9_W, 10_W, 11_W — Standard English Conventions 1-3)

**Subject-Verb Agreement**
- Singular subject → singular verb; plural → plural
- **Tricky**: Collective nouns (team, group) = singular on SAT/ACT
- **Intervening phrases**: "The box of chocolates *is* open" (box is subject)
- **Compound subjects**: "A and B *are*"; "A or B *is/are* (match closest)"

**Pronouns**
- **Case**: Subject (I, he, she, we, they) vs Object (me, him, her, us, them)
- **Agreement**: Antecedent singular → singular pronoun (he/she/it); plural → they
- **Ambiguity**: "John told Mark he failed" → who failed? → Revise for clarity
- **Who vs Whom**: Who = subject; Whom = object (test: replace with he/him)

**Verb Tense & Aspect**
- **Consistency**: Stay in same timeframe unless shift is logical
- **Perfect tenses**: Have/has/had + past participle (completed action relative to another time)
- **Would/Could/Should + have + past participle** (conditional perfect)

**Modifiers** (from 5_Modifiers.pdf, 5_(Ans)_Modifiers.pdf)
- **Misplaced**: Modifier too far from word it modifies → move it
- **Dangling**: No logical subject in sentence → add subject or rewrite
- **Participial phrases** (-ing/-ed): Must modify subject of main clause

**Parallel Structure**
- List items: Same grammatical form (all -ing, all infinitives, all nouns)
- Correlative pairs: Either...or, neither...nor, not only...but also → parallel elements

**Comparisons**
- **Logical**: Compare like things (apples to apples)
- **Form**: -er/more for 2 things; -est/most for 3+
- **Double comparisons**: "more better" ❌; "most unique" ❌ (unique is absolute)

#### Punctuation (from 14_Commas, Dashes, Colons.pdf, 14_(Ans)_Commas, Dashes, Colons.pdf)

| Mark | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **Comma** | Before coordinating conjunction (FANBOYS) joining independent clauses | I studied, **but** I forgot. |
| | After introductory element | **However,** the test was hard. |
| | Between coordinate adjectives | A **long, difficult** test. |
| | Non-essential clause (appositive) | My friend**,** a doctor**,** called. |
| **Semicolon** | Between independent clauses (no conjunction) | I studied; I passed. |
| | Complex list (items contain commas) | Cities: Paris, France; Rome, Italy. |
| **Colon** | After independent clause → list/explanation/quote | Three things: **pen, paper, focus.** |
| **Dash** | Emphasis, interruption, summary | He passed—**against all odds.** |
| **Apostrophe** | Possession (singular: 's; plural ending in s: s') | The student's book; the students' books |
| | Contractions | It's = it is; Its = possessive |

#### Sentence Boundaries (from Boundaries_L2, Form_L2)
- **Run-on**: Two independent clauses, no punctuation → add period, semicolon, or comma+FANBOYS
- **Comma Splice**: Two independent clauses, only comma → upgrade punctuation
- **Fragment**: Incomplete thought (no subject/verb, or subordinate clause alone) → attach or complete

- "At this point in time" → "Now"
