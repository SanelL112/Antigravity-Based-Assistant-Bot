## 📚 Overview

Lines and angles form the **foundation of SAT Geometry** (~15% of Math section in Geometry & Trigonometry domain). These concepts appear both as standalone questions and as building blocks for triangles, circles, and coordinate geometry problems.

**Key Topics:**
- Parallel lines & transversals
- Angle relationships (vertical, supplementary, complementary)
- Triangle angle theorems
- Polygon interior/exterior angles
- Perpendicular & angle bisectors
- Coordinate geometry applications

## 🎯 Essential Theorems & Properties

### Basic Angle Relationships

| Relationship | Definition | Sum/Equality |
|--------------|------------|--------------|
| **Vertical Angles** | Opposite angles formed by intersecting lines | **Equal** |
| **Linear Pair** | Adjacent angles on a line | **Supplementary (180°)** |
| **Complementary** | Two angles summing to 90° | Sum = 90° |
| **Supplementary** | Two angles summing to 180° | Sum = 180° |
| **Angle Bisector** | Ray dividing angle into two equal parts | Creates two equal angles |

---

### Parallel Lines & Transversals

When a **transversal** crosses **parallel lines**:

| Angle Pair | Relationship | Example |
|------------|--------------|---------|
| **Corresponding** | Same position at each intersection | ∠1 = ∠5, ∠2 = ∠6, ∠3 = ∠7, ∠4 = ∠8 |
| **Alternate Interior** | Inside, opposite sides of transversal | ∠3 = ∠6, ∠4 = ∠5 |
| **Alternate Exterior** | Outside, opposite sides of transversal | ∠1 = ∠8, ∠2 = ∠7 |
| **Same-Side Interior** | Inside, same side of transversal | ∠3 + ∠5 = 180°, ∠4 + ∠6 = 180° |
| **Same-Side Exterior** | Outside, same side of transversal | ∠1 + ∠7 = 180°, ∠2 + ∠8 = 180° |

**Memory Aid:** "Corresponding = Equal, Alternate = Equal, Same-Side = Supplementary"

## 🔺 Triangle Angle Theorems

| Theorem | Statement | Application |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **Triangle Sum** | ∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180° | Find missing angle |
| **Exterior Angle** | Exterior ∠ = Sum of 2 remote interior ∠s | ∠EXT = ∠A + ∠B |
| **Isosceles Triangle** | Base angles are equal | If AB = AC, then ∠B = ∠C |
| **Equilateral Triangle** | All angles = 60° | All sides equal |
| **Triangle Inequality** | Sum of any 2 sides > 3rd side | a + b > c, |a - b| < c |
| **Hinge Theorem** | Larger angle → longer opposite side | Compare sides via angles |
| **Angle Bisector** | Divides opposite side proportionally | AB/AC = BD/DC |

### Special Right Triangles (MEMORIZE)

| Triangle | Angles | Side Ratio | Area |
|----------|--------|------------|------|
| **45-45-90** | 45°, 45°, 90° | x : x : x√2 | x²/2 |
| **30-60-90** | 30°, 60°, 90° | x : x√3 : 2x | x²√3/2 |
| **3-4-5** | ~37°, ~53°, 90° | 3k : 4k : 5k | 6k² |
| **5-12-13** | ~23°, ~67°, 90° | 5k : 12k : 13k | 30k² |

## 📐 Polygon Angle Formulas

| Polygon | Interior Sum | Each Interior (Regular) | Each Exterior (Regular) |
|---------|--------------|-------------------------|-------------------------|
| Triangle | 180° | 60° | 120° |
| Quadrilateral | 360° | 90° | 90° |
| Pentagon | 540° | 108° | 72° |
| Hexagon | 720° | 120° | 60° |
| n-gon | **(n-2) × 180°** | **(n-2) × 180° / n** | **360° / n** |

**Key:** Sum of exterior angles (one per vertex) = **360°** for ANY polygon.

## 📏 Perpendicular & Angle Bisectors

### Perpendicular Bisector Theorem
- **Any point on the perpendicular bisector of a segment is equidistant from the endpoints.**
- **Converse:** If a point is equidistant from endpoints, it lies on the perpendicular bisector.

### Angle Bisector Theorem
- **In △ABC, if AD bisects ∠A, then AB/AC = BD/DC**
- **Coordinates of incenter:** Weighted average by side lengths

### Triangle Centers (Coordinate Geometry)

| Center | Construction | Coordinates (A, B, C vertices) | Key Property |
|--------|--------------|--------------------------------|--------------|
| **Centroid** | Medians intersect | ((x₁+x₂+x₃)/3, (y₁+y₂+y₃)/3) | Divides medians 2:1 |
| **Circumcenter** | Perpendicular bisectors | Equidistant from vertices | Center of circumscribed circle |
| **Incenter** | Angle bisectors | Weighted by side lengths | Center of inscribed circle |
| **Orthocenter** | Altitudes intersect | — | Acute: inside; Right: at right angle; Obtuse: outside |

**Euler Line:** Centroid, circumcenter, orthocenter are **collinear**. Centroid is ⅔ from orthocenter to circumcenter. Only equilateral triangles have all centers coincide.

## 📐 Coordinate Geometry with Lines & Angles

### Slope & Angle Relationships

| Concept | Formula | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| **Slope** | m = (y₂ - y₁)/(x₂ - x₁) | Rise over run |
| **Parallel Lines** | m₁ = m₂ | Same slope |
| **Perpendicular Lines** | m₁ × m₂ = -1 | Negative reciprocals |
| **Angle Between Lines** | tan θ = |(m₂ - m₁)/(1 + m₁m₂)| | Acute angle θ |

### Distance & Midpoint
- **Distance:** d = √[(x₂-x₁)² + (y₂-y₁)²]
- **Midpoint:** ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2)

### Line Equations
| Form | Equation | When to Use |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **Slope-Intercept** | y = mx + b | Given slope & y-intercept |
| **Point-Slope** | y - y₁ = m(x - x₁) | Given point & slope |
| **Standard** | Ax + By = C | Given intercepts, integer coefficients |
| **Two-Point** | y - y₁ = (y₂-y₁)/(x₂-x₁)(x - x₁) | Given two points |

## 🧮 Practice Problems

### Problem 1: Parallel Lines
**Lines l and m are parallel.** Transversal t creates ∠1 = 3x + 10, ∠2 = 5x - 30. ∠1 and ∠2 are corresponding angles.
**Find x.**

**Solution:**
Corresponding angles are equal: 3x + 10 = 5x - 30
40 = 2x → **x = 20**
∠1 = ∠2 = 70°

---

### Problem 2: Triangle Exterior Angle
**△ABC**, ∠A = 40°, ∠B = 70°. Exterior angle at C extends side BC.
**Find the exterior angle.**

**Solution:**
Exterior ∠ = ∠A + ∠B = 40° + 70° = **110°**
(Also: Interior ∠C = 180° - 110° = 70°, Exterior = 180° - 70° = 110° ✓)

---

### Problem 3: Isosceles Triangle
**△ABC**, AB = AC, ∠A = 40°.
**Find ∠B and ∠C.**

**Solution:**
Base angles equal: ∠B = ∠C
∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°
40° + 2∠B = 180°
2∠B = 140° → **∠B = ∠C = 70°**

---

### Problem 4: 30-60-90 Triangle
**Right triangle**, short leg = 6, opposite 30°.
**Find:** Long leg, hypotenuse, area.

**Solution:**
Ratio: x : x√3 : 2x
Short leg = x = 6
Long leg = 6√3
Hypotenuse = 12
Area = ½ × 6 × 6√3 = **18√3**

---

### Problem 5: Polygon Interior Angle
**Regular polygon** has interior angle of 150°.
**Find number of sides.**

**Solution:**
Interior = (n-2) × 180° / n = 150°
180n - 360 = 150n
30n = 360
**n = 12** (dodecagon)

Check: Exterior = 180° - 150° = 30°. 360°/30° = 12 ✓

---

### Problem 6: Perpendicular Bisector
**Segment endpoints:** A(2, 3), B(8, 7).
**Find:** Equation of perpendicular bisector.

**Solution:**
Midpoint: ((2+8)/2, (3+7)/2) = (5, 5)
Slope AB: (7-3)/(8-2) = 4/6 = 2/3
Perpendicular slope: -3/2
Equation: y - 5 = -3/2(x - 5)
**y = -3/2 x + 25/2**

---

### Problem 7: Distance from Point to Line
**Line:** 3x + 4y = 12
**Point:** P(1, 1)
**Find:** Shortest distance from P to line.

**Solution:**
Distance = |Ax₀ + By₀ + C| / √(A² + B²)
Line: 3x + 4y - 12 = 0
Distance = |3(1) + 4(1) - 12| / √(9 + 16)
= |3 + 4 - 12| / 5 = 5/5 = **1**

---

### Problem 8: Angle Between Lines
**Line 1:** y = 2x + 1 (m₁ = 2)
**Line 2:** y = -1/2 x + 3 (m₂ = -1/2)
**Find:** Acute angle between lines.

**Solution:**
m₁ × m₂ = 2 × (-1/2) = -1 → **Lines are perpendicular**
Angle = **90°**

---

### Problem 9: Triangle Inequality
**Sides of triangle:** 7, 10, x (integer).
**Find:** Possible integer values of x.

**Solution:**
7 + 10 > x → x < 17
|10 - 7| < x → x > 3
**x ∈ {4, 5, 6, ..., 16}** → **13 possible values**

---

### Problem 10: Centroid Coordinates
**△ABC:** A(0, 0), B(6, 0), C(0, 9).
**Find:** Centroid coordinates.

**Solution:**
Centroid = ((x₁+x₂+x₃)/3, (y₁+y₂+y₃)/3)
= ((0+6+0)/3, (0+0+9)/3)
= **(2, 3)**

## 🎯 Problem-Solving Strategies

### Strategy 1: Angle Chase
1. **Mark all given angles** on diagram
2. **Apply triangle sum** (180°) wherever possible
3. **Use parallel line theorems** (corresponding, alternate interior)
4. **Look for linear pairs** (180°) and vertical angles (equal)
5. **Work backwards** from target angle

### Strategy 2: Coordinate Geometry Setup
1. **Plot key points** if not given
2. **Find slopes** for parallel/perpendicular checks
3. **Use distance formula** for side lengths
4. **Complete the square** for circle equations
5. **Use Desmos** to verify intersections

### Strategy 3: Special Triangle Recognition
- **Isosceles?** → Base angles equal
- **Right triangle?** → Pythagorean theorem, special ratios
- **30-60-90?** → Short leg opposite 30°, hypotenuse = 2×short
- **45-45-90?** → Legs equal, hypotenuse = leg×√2

### Strategy 4: Exterior Angle Shortcut
**Exterior angle = Sum of two remote interior angles**
Often faster than finding interior angle first.

### Strategy 5: Polygon Formula
**Interior angle = (n-2)×180°/n**
**Exterior angle = 360°/n**
**Interior + Exterior = 180°**

## 🎯 DSAT-Specific Tips

### Desmos Strategies
1. **Graph lines:** y = mx + b
2. **Find intersections:** Click intersection points
3. **Angle measurement:** Use `arctan` for slope → angle
4. **Distance:** Type `distance((x1,y1),(x2,y2))`
5. **Sliders:** `m = 2` → add slider for dynamic exploration

### Time-Saving Patterns
- **Vertical angles** → instant equality
- **Parallel lines** → corresponding/alternate = equal
- **Triangle sum** → 180° always
- **30-60-90 / 45-45-90** → side ratios instantly
- **Exterior angle** → sum of remote interiors

### Common Traps
| Trap | Example | Defense |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Assuming parallel** | "Lines look parallel" | Must be stated or proven |
| **Confusing interior/exterior** | Using 180° - interior for exterior | Exterior = remote interior sum |
| **Degrees vs Radians** | Arc length formula | Check units; SAT usually degrees |
| **Midpoint vs Centroid** | Midpoint of side vs centroid of triangle | Centroid = average of vertices |
| **Slope sign** | Perpendicular = -1/m | Negative reciprocal, not just negative |

## ⚠️ Error Checklist

- [ ] Are lines stated as parallel? (Don't assume!)
- [ ] Did I use 180° for triangle sum?
- [ ] Is angle central or inscribed? (Factor of 2)
- [ ] For exterior angle: Sum of remote interiors?
- [ ] Special triangle: Correct side opposite correct angle?
- [ ] Perpendicular slope: Negative RECIPROCAL, not just negative?
- [ ] Polygon: Interior vs exterior formula?
- [ ] Coordinates: Order in distance/midpoint formula?
- [ ] Units: Degrees or radians?
