## What is Factoring?

Factoring means **breaking a polynomial into a product of simpler polynomials** (called factors) that, when multiplied together, give you the original.

For quadratics, this means:
$$ax^2 + bx + c = (mx + p)(nx + q)$$

**Why does the SAT care?**
- To find the **roots/zeros** of a function (x-intercepts of the graph)
- To simplify rational expressions
- To solve quadratic equations
- To convert to vertex form by completing the square

## AC Method

The most systematic approach when a ≠ 1:

### Steps:
1. **Multiply a × c** (leading coefficient × constant term)
2. **Find two numbers** with:
   - Product = a × c
   - Sum = b (the middle coefficient)
3. **Split the middle term** using those two numbers
4. **Factor by grouping** (take GCF of first pair and second pair)
5. **Combine** the common binomial

### Example 1: 4x² - 8x + 3
- a × c = 4 × 3 = **12**
- Find product = 12, sum = -8 → **-6** and **-2**
- Split: 4x² - 6x - 2x + 3
- Group: 2x(2x - 3) - 1(2x - 3)
- Answer: **(2x - 1)(2x - 3)**

### Example 2: 6x² + 7x - 49
- a × c = 6 × (-49) = **-294**
- Find product = -294, sum = 7 → **21** and **-14**
- Split: 6x² + 21x - 14x - 49
- Group: 3x(2x + 7) - 7(2x + 7)
- Answer: **(3x - 7)(2x + 7)**

### Example 3: 10x² + 17x + 3
- a × c = 10 × 3 = **30**
- Find product = 30, sum = 17 → **15** and **2**
- Split: 10x² + 15x + 2x + 3
- Group: 5x(2x + 3) + 1(2x + 3)
- Answer: **(5x + 1)(2x + 3)**

## Trial and Error Method

When the numbers are small, you can "guess and check":

1. List factor pairs of a
2. List factor pairs of c
3. Test combinations until FOIL gives you the original middle term

### Example: 15x² - 34x + 15
- Factor pairs of 15: (1,15) or (3,5)
- Factor pairs of 15: (1,15), (3,5), (5,3), (15,1)
- Testing: (3x - 5)(5x - 3) = 15x² - 9x - 25x + 15 = 15x² - 34x + 15 ✓

## Special Patterns

### 1. Difference of Squares
$$a^2 - b^2 = (a-b)(a+b)$$

**Example:** 16x² - 49 = **(4x - 7)(4x + 7)**
**Example:** 25x² - 9 = **(5x - 3)(5x + 3)**

### 2. Perfect Square Trinomials
$$a^2 + 2ab + b^2 = (a+b)^2$$
$$a^2 - 2ab + b^2 = (a-b)^2$$

**Example:** 4x² - 12x + 9 = **(2x - 3)²**

⚠️ **Watch out:** (2x - 3)² ≠ 4x² - 9 ! The middle term -12x must be present.

### 3. Common GCF First
Always factor out the GCF before factoring the trinomial!

**Example:** 6x² - 12x - 16 = **2(3x² - 6x - 8)**

## SAT Traps & Common Mistakes

1. **Forgetting to check for GCF first** — always look for a common factor
2. **Sign errors** — negative c means OPPOSITE signs in the factors; positive c means SAME signs
3. **Mixed signs after splitting** — be careful with subtraction in the grouping step
4. **Not checking by FOIL** — always verify (First + Outer + Inner + Last = original?)
5. **Giving up too quickly** — if GCF+AC method doesn't work immediately, try Trial and Error
6. **"False factor" trap** — some trinomials in the practice set look unfactorable but ARE (100% factorable in this set)

## 📌 Quick Reference: Factoring Decision Tree

```
Is there a GCF?
├── YES: Factor it out first
└── NO: Is a = 1?
├── YES: Find two numbers with product = c, sum = b → (x + m)(x + n)
└── NO (a ≠ 1):
├── Try AC Method (multiply a×c, find factors, split, group)
├── Check for special patterns (diff of squares, perfect square)
└── If stuck: Trial and Error with factor pairs
```
