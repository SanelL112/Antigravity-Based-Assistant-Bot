## 📐 CORE CIRCLE FORMULAS (Memorize These!)

| Formula | Expression | When to Use |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| **Area** | A = πr² | Given radius/diameter, find area |
| **Circumference** | C = 2πr = πd | Given radius/diameter, find perimeter |
| **Arc Length** | L = (θ/360) × 2πr | Given central angle θ (degrees) |
| **Sector Area** | A = (θ/360) × πr² | Given central angle θ (degrees) |
| **Equation of Circle** | (x - h)² + (y - k)² = r² | Center (h,k), radius r |

> **SAT Reference Sheet provides:** Area and Circumference formulas only. You MUST memorize Arc Length, Sector Area, and Equation of Circle.

## 🎯 KEY CIRCLE THEOREMS (Tested Frequently)

| # | Theorem | Statement | Diagram Key |
|---|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **1** | **Central Angle = Intercepted Arc** | m∠AOB = measure of arc AB | ∠AOB at center |
| **2** | **Inscribed Angle = ½ × Intercepted Arc** | m∠ACB = ½ × measure of arc AB | ∠ACB on circle |
| **3** | **Inscribed Angle in Semicircle = 90°** | If AB is diameter, ∠ACB = 90° | Thales' Theorem |
| **4** | **Tangent ⟂ Radius at Point of Tangency** | Radius to tangent point forms 90° | Right angle mark |
| **5** | **Congruent Chords ↔ Congruent Arcs** | Equal chords cut equal arcs | Chord lengths |
| **6** | **Radius ⟂ Chord → Bisects Chord** | Perpendicular from center bisects chord | Midpoint |
| **7** | **Opposite Angles of Cyclic Quadrilateral** | Sum to 180° | Quadrilateral in circle |
| **8** | **Angle Formed by Chord & Tangent** | = ½ × intercepted arc | Tangent-chord angle |

## 🧮 PROBLEM TYPES & SOLUTION PATTERNS

### Type 1: Equation of a Circle → Center & Radius
**Given:** x² + y² - 6x + 8y = 0  
**Find:** Center and radius  

**Steps:**
1. Group x and y terms: (x² - 6x) + (y² + 8y) = 0
2. Complete the square:
   - x: (6/2)² = 9 → add 9
   - y: (8/2)² = 16 → add 16
3. (x² - 6x + 9) + (y² + 8y + 16) = 0 + 9 + 16
4. (x - 3)² + (y + 4)² = 25
5. **Center: (3, -4), Radius: 5**

> ⚠️ **Watch for:** x² + y² + Dx + Ey + F = 0 form. Complete the square ALWAYS.

---

### Type 2: Arc Length & Sector Area
**Given:** Circle radius 10, central angle 72°  
**Find:** Arc length and sector area  

**Arc Length:** L = (72/360) × 2π(10) = (1/5) × 20π = **4π**  
**Sector Area:** A = (72/360) × π(10)² = (1/5) × 100π = **20π**

> 💡 **Shortcut:** Fraction = θ/360. Multiply by circumference (2πr) or area (πr²).

---

### Type 3: Inscribed Angle Problems
**Given:** Inscribed angle = 35°  
**Find:** Intercepted arc measure  

**Solution:** Arc = 2 × 35° = **70°**

**Given:** Central angle = 120°  
**Find:** Inscribed angle intercepting same arc  

**Solution:** Inscribed = ½ × 120° = **60°**

---

### Type 4: Circle + Right Triangle (VERY COMMON)
**Pattern:** Right triangle inscribed in circle → Hypotenuse = Diameter

**Example:** Right triangle with legs 6 and 8 inscribed in circle. Find circle area.  
1. Hypotenuse = √(6² + 8²) = 10 = **diameter**
2. Radius = 5
3. Area = π(5)² = **25π**

---

### Type 5: Tangent Problems
**Tangent-Radius:** 90° angle at point of tangency  
**Two Tangents from External Point:** Equal lengths  
**Tangent-Chord Angle:** = ½ × intercepted arc

**Example:** From point P outside circle, tangents PA and PB. PA = 12. Find PB.  
**Answer:** **12** (tangents from same external point are congruent)

---

### Type 6: Chord-Chord / Secant-Secant / Secant-Tangent Power Theorems
*(Rare but tested on harder questions)*

| Configuration | Formula |
|---------------|---------|
| **Two Chords Intersecting** | AE × EB = CE × ED |
| **Two Secants from External Point** | PA × PB = PC × PD |
| **Secant & Tangent from External Point** | PT² = PA × PB |

## 📝 PRACTICE PROBLEMS (from 19_Circles.pdf & Class Materials)

### Set A: Equation of Circle
1. **Find center and radius:** x² + y² + 10x - 4y = 20  
   *Answer: Center (-5, 2), Radius = 7*

2. **Write equation:** Center (2, -3), passes through (5, 1)  
   *Answer: (x-2)² + (y+3)² = 25 (r = √(3²+4²) = 5)*

3. **Circle has diameter endpoints (1,2) and (7,8). Find equation.**  
   *Answer: Center (4,5), r = √18 = 3√2 → (x-4)² + (y-5)² = 18*

### Set B: Arc Length & Sector Area
4. **Radius 12, central angle 150°. Find arc length and sector area.**  
   *Arc = (150/360)×24π = 10π; Sector = (150/360)×144π = 60π*

5. **Sector area = 18π, central angle = 60°. Find radius.**  
   *(60/360)πr² = 18π → (1/6)r² = 18 → r² = 108 → r = 6√3*

6. **Arc length = 8π, radius = 10. Find central angle.**  
   *(θ/360)×20π = 8π → θ/360 = 2/5 → θ = 144°*

### Set C: Inscribed Angles & Theorems
7. **In circle O, ∠AOB = 110° (central). Find ∠ACB (inscribed, same arc).**  
   *Answer: 55°*

8. **Inscribed angle intercepts arc of 130°. Angle measure?**  
   *Answer: 65°*

9. **Triangle ABC inscribed in circle, AB is diameter, ∠C = 90°. AC = 6, BC = 8. Find circle area.**  
   *AB = 10 (3-4-5 triple) → r = 5 → Area = 25π*

### Set D: Tangents & Chords
10. **PA tangent at A, PB tangent at B, PA = 15. Find PB.**  
    *Answer: 15*

11. **Chords AB and CD intersect at E. AE = 6, EB = 4, CE = 3. Find ED.**  
    *AE×EB = CE×ED → 6×4 = 3×ED → ED = 8*

12. **From point P outside circle, tangent PT = 10, secant PAB with PA = 4. Find PB.**  
    *PT² = PA×PB → 100 = 4×PB → PB = 25*

### Set E: Coordinate Geometry with Circles
13. **Circle: (x+2)² + (y-5)² = 36. Does point (4, 5) lie inside, on, or outside?**  
    *Distance from center (-2,5) to (4,5) = 6 = radius → ON the circle*

14. **Find intersection of circle (x-3)² + y² = 25 and line y = 4.**  
    *(x-3)² + 16 = 25 → (x-3)² = 9 → x-3 = ±3 → x = 0 or 6*  
    *Points: (0,4) and (6,4)*

## 🎯 SAT-SPECIFIC STRATEGIES

### 1. **Always Draw the Diagram**
- Label center, radii, given angles/lengths
- Mark right angles (tangent-radius, inscribed in semicircle)
- Extend lines to create triangles if needed

### 2. **Use the Radius as Your Friend**
- Draw radii to tangency points, chord endpoints, triangle vertices
- Creates isosceles triangles (two radii = equal sides)
- Creates right triangles (radius ⟂ tangent)

### 3. **Equation of Circle = Completing the Square**
- If you see x² + y² + Dx + Ey + F = 0 → Complete the square
- Center = (-D/2, -E/2), Radius = √(D²/4 + E²/4 - F)

### 4. **Proportional Thinking for Arcs/Sectors**
- Fraction = central angle / 360
- Arc length = fraction × circumference
- Sector area = fraction × circle area

### 5. **Look for Special Right Triangles**
- 3-4-5, 5-12-13, 8-15-17, 45-45-90, 30-60-90
- Diameter = hypotenuse of inscribed right triangle

### 6. **Desmos Verification**
- Graph circle: (x-h)² + (y-k)² = r²
- Graph line: y = mx + b
- Find intersection points
- Measure distances/angles

## 🚀 ADVANCED: HARD SAT CIRCLE QUESTIONS

### Problem Type: Circle + Algebra
**"A circle has equation x² + y² - 8x + 2y = 8. A line through the center with slope 3 intersects the circle at points A and B. Find AB."**

**Solution:**
1. Complete square: (x-4)² + (y+1)² = 25 → Center (4,-1), r=5
2. Line through center: y + 1 = 3(x - 4) → y = 3x - 13
3. AB is a **diameter** (line through center) → AB = 2r = **10**

> 💡 Any line through center = diameter = 2r. No algebra needed!

---

### Problem Type: Inscribed Quadrilateral
**"Quadrilateral ABCD is inscribed in a circle. ∠A = 70°, ∠B = 110°. Find ∠C and ∠D."**

**Solution:**
- Opposite angles sum to 180°
- ∠C = 180° - ∠A = 110°
- ∠D = 180° - ∠B = 70°

---

### Problem Type: Circle + Coordinate Distance
**"Circle centered at (3, -2) passes through (7, 1). What is the area?"**

**Solution:**
- r = distance = √[(7-3)² + (1-(-2))²] = √[16 + 9] = 5
- Area = π(5)² = **25π**

## ❌ COMMON TRAPS TO AVOID

| Trap | Wrong Thinking | Correct Approach |
|------|----------------|------------------|
| **Diameter vs Radius** | Using d in πr² | Area uses r; C uses d or r |
| **Central vs Inscribed** | Confusing which is double | Central = Arc; Inscribed = ½ Arc |
| **Equation of Circle** | Forgetting to complete square | ALWAYS complete square for general form |
| **Arc Length Formula** | Using θ in radians | SAT uses degrees: (θ/360) × 2πr |
| **Tangent Length** | Assuming tangent = radius | Tangent length from external point ≠ radius |
| **Inscribed Right Triangle** | Not recognizing hypotenuse = diameter | If right triangle in circle → hypotenuse = diameter |
