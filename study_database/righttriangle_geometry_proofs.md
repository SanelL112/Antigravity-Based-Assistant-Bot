### General Summary: Right Triangle Geometry Proofs

**Key Formulas**
1. **Pythagorean Theorem:** $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$  
   *(where $a$ and $b$ are the legs, and $c$ is the hypotenuse)*
2. **Trigonometric Ratios (SOH-CAH-TOA):**  
   - $\sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$  
   - $\cos(\theta) = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$  
   - $\tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}$
3. **Area of a Right Triangle:** $A = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{leg}_1 \times \text{leg}_2$
4. **Geometric Mean (Altitude Rule):** If an altitude $h$ is drawn to the hypotenuse, splitting it into segments $p$ and $q$, then $h^2 = p \times q$ and $c = p + q$.

**Key Facts**
1. A right triangle has exactly one $90^\circ$ (right) angle.
2. The **hypotenuse** is the side opposite the right angle and is always the longest side.
3. **Congruence Theorems for Right Triangles** (in addition to standard triangle congruences):  
   - **HL (Hypotenuse-Leg):** If the hypotenuse and one leg of a right triangle are congruent to those of another, the triangles are congruent.  
   - **LL (Leg-Leg):** Equivalent to SAS congruence.  
   - **HA (Hypotenuse-Acute Angle):** Equivalent to AAS congruence.  
   - **LA (Leg-Acute Angle):** Equivalent to ASA or SAA congruence.
4. **Similarity:** Drawing an altitude from the right angle to the hypotenuse creates two smaller triangles that are similar to the original triangle and to each other.

**Examples**
1. **Pythagorean Triple Proof:** Showing that a triangle with sides 3, 4, and 5 is a right triangle because $3^2 + 4^2 = 9 + 16 = 25 = 5^2$.
2. **HL Congruence Proof:** Given $\triangle ABC$ and $\triangle DEF$ with right angles at $B$ and $E$, if $AC \cong DF$ (hypotenuses) and $AB \cong DE$ (legs), then $\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEF$.
3. **Altitude/Geometric Mean Example:** In a right triangle, if the altitude to the hypotenuse divides it into segments of length 4 and 9, the altitude length is $\sqrt{4 \times 9} = 6$, and the hypotenuse is $4 + 9 = 13$.
